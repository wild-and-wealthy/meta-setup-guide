KPI Tracking Guide

What to track, what the numbers mean, and when to act.

Use with: Your KPI Tracker template
Review: Daily while ads are running
Applies to: All ad types

01 /

What Are KPIs — and Why Do They Matter?

KPI stands for Key Performance Indicator — a specific, measurable number that tells you how a particular step in your funnel is performing.

Without KPIs, you're guessing. You might know your ads are running and leads are coming in, but you have no way to know where your funnel is strong, where it's leaking, or what to fix first. KPIs remove the guesswork.

Every number in your tracker tells a story about one specific stage — from the moment someone sees your ad, to the moment they buy. When a KPI drops below its baseline, you know exactly where the problem is and what to look at.

The goal isn't to monitor every number every day. Your job is to glance at the tracker daily, spot anything below baseline, and know immediately what that means and where to look. That's the whole system.

There are two types of KPIs in your tracker:

  • Input metrics — numbers you enter manually (spend, leads, booked calls, closed deals, etc.)
  • Calculated metrics — percentages and costs that your tracker computes automatically from the inputs (CTR, conversion rates, cost per lead, etc.)

You only need to fill in the input columns. The tracker does the rest.

Baseline Metrics — Minimum Performance Standards

Baseline metrics are the minimum acceptable levels for each key step in your funnel. They are not ideal targets — they are the floor. If any metric falls below its baseline, it signals a specific bottleneck that needs attention.

How to use this section: check your tracker daily. Any number below its baseline is a signal, not a crisis. Use the "what to improve" notes to know exactly where to look.
Baseline 01
CTR (All)
>6% Minimum

The percentage of total impressions that result in any type of click on your ad — including link clicks, profile visits, likes, and shares.

If below 6% → the ad creative or hook needs work.

  1. Your verbal hook (the first line you say) isn't compelling enough to stop the scroll
  2. The opening visual or scene isn't eye-catching enough to grab attention
Baseline 02
CTR (Link)
>3% Minimum

The percentage of impressions that result in a link click — someone actually clicking through to your landing page or funnel.

If below 3% → the body of your ad or call to action needs work.

  1. Your ad isn't demonstrating enough value to keep the viewer watching through to the CTA
  2. Your call to action is weak — either the language or what you're offering in exchange for the click
Baseline 03
Lead Conversion Rate
>20% Minimum

The percentage of landing page visitors who become leads by opting in, submitting a form, or registering.

If below 20% → the landing page messaging, layout, or offer needs work.

  1. The headline isn't making a bold enough promise — visitors don't see enough value to give their information
  2. The design, layout, or color scheme is too busy or unclear

Applies to direct-to-landing-page ads. Not applicable for DM or instant form campaigns.

Baseline 04
Saw Offer Conversion Rate
>20% Minimum

The percentage of people who stayed in the webclass long enough to actually see the sales offer.

If below 20% → webinar retention, delivery, or engagement needs work.

  1. If drop-off happens early: your hook or bold promise isn't strong enough to retain attention
  2. If drop-off happens mid-section: the content is either boring or not valuable enough
  3. If drop-off happens just before the pitch: your transition into the offer is too weak

Applies to direct-to-landing-page / webinar funnels.

Baseline 05
Front End Sale Conversion Rate
>3% Minimum

The percentage of people who saw the offer and purchased the front-end product.

If below 3% → offer clarity, pitch strength, or perceived value needs improvement.

  1. Your offer is too weak — people don't see enough value relative to the price
  2. Not enough social proof — people aren't convinced it will work for them
Baseline 06
Booked Call Rate
>70% Minimum

The percentage of eligible front-end buyers who book a strategy call.

If below 70% → your Discovery Process, posture, or urgency needs attention.

  1. Your Discovery Process isn't creating enough perceived value for the call
  2. Posture issue — leads feel like they are the prize, not you
  3. Not enough urgency — people believe they can book later (they won't)
Baseline 07
Show Rate
>80% Minimum

The percentage of scheduled calls the prospect actually showed up to.

If below 80% → reminder workflows, confirmation messaging, or scheduling friction need work.

  1. Not enough posture around the call — e.g., "If you miss your call, we won't let you rebook"
  2. Not enough reminders leading up to the call (email, text, phone call sequence)
Baseline 08
Back-End Sale Conversion Rate (Close Rate)
>10% Minimum

The percentage of attended calls that convert into a back-end sale.

If below 10% → lead quality, sales process, or objection handling needs improvement.

  1. If you're hearing a lot of "I can't afford it" — you may have a lead quality issue upstream
  2. Most likely: a skills gap in the sales conversation itself — discovery, objection handling, or the close

Tracking Sheet Glossary + Calculations

Every column in your KPI tracker is defined below, including how it's calculated. Columns marked as calculated are filled in automatically by formulas — you only need to enter the raw input numbers.

Input vs. Calculated columns — Input columns are ones you fill in manually (spend, leads, booked calls, etc.). Calculated columns are generated by your tracker automatically and you should never overwrite them.
Attended
The number of people who showed up to the booked call, discovery call, or appointment. Input column.
Book Call Rate
The percentage of eligible front-end buyers who booked a call. Calculated.
Book Call Rate = Booked Calls ÷ Front End Sales (buyers who entered the Discovery Process)
Booked Calls
The total number of calls scheduled with prospects. Input column.
Clicks (All)
The total number of clicks on the ad — including link clicks, profile visits, likes, shares, and any other interaction. Pulled from Meta Ads Manager. Input column.
Close Rate
The percentage of attended calls that resulted in a closed sale. Calculated.
Close Rate = Closed Deals ÷ Attended Calls
Closed
The total number of back-end deals closed (sales won) during the period. Input column.
Commission
The total commission earned from closed deals during the period. Input column.
Completed Webclass / Saw Offer
The number of people who stayed in the webclass long enough to see the sales offer. Used interchangeably with "Saw Offer" depending on how your funnel tracks it. Input column.
Completes Discovery Process
The number of front-end buyers who completed the full Discovery Process (form, questionnaire, or multi-step qualifier). Input column.
Connection Rate
The percentage of new DMs that turned into a meaningful two-way conversation (a Connection). Calculated.
Connection Rate = Connections ÷ New DMs
Connections
The number of people who responded meaningfully to a DM and progressed into a real conversation — not just a reply, but genuine engagement. Input column.
Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)
The average ad spend required to generate one front-end sale. Calculated.
CPA = Total Ad Spend ÷ Front End Sales
Cost Per Booked Call
The average ad spend required to generate one booked strategy call. Calculated.
Cost Per Booked Call = Total Ad Spend ÷ Booked Calls
Cost Per Connection
The average cost to generate one Connection in a DM funnel. Calculated.
Cost Per Connection = Total Ad Spend ÷ Connections
Cost Per DM
The average ad cost to generate one new direct message conversation. Calculated.
Cost Per DM = Total Ad Spend ÷ New DMs
Cost Per Lead
The average ad cost to generate one lead (opt-in, registration, or survey submission). Calculated.
Cost Per Lead = Total Ad Spend ÷ Leads
Cost Per MQL
The average ad cost to generate one Marketing Qualified Lead. Calculated.
Cost Per MQL = Total Ad Spend ÷ MQLs
Cost Per Saw Offer
The average ad cost required to get one person to the point of seeing your offer in the webclass. Calculated.
Cost Per Saw Offer = Total Ad Spend ÷ People Who Saw Offer
CPM
Cost per 1,000 impressions. Tells you how expensive it is to reach your audience. Pulled from Meta. Input column.
CPM = Total Ad Spend ÷ (Total Impressions ÷ 1,000)
CTR (All)
The percentage of impressions that resulted in any click on your ad — including all interaction types. Calculated.
CTR (All) = Clicks (All) ÷ Impressions
CTR (Link)
The percentage of impressions that resulted in someone clicking through to your landing page or funnel. Calculated.
CTR (Link) = Link Clicks ÷ Impressions
Date
The day, week, or reporting period the data represents. Enter each day as its own row. Input column.
Discovery Process Completion Rate
The percentage of front-end buyers who completed the full Discovery Process. Calculated.
Discovery Process Completion Rate = Completes Discovery Process ÷ Front End Sales
DM Response Rate
The percentage of new DMs that received a reply from the lead (first reply only). Calculated.
DM Response Rate = DM Responses ÷ New DMs
DM Responses
The number of people who replied to your DM — counting first replies only, not the full conversation. Input column.
Earnings Per Appointment
The average commission earned per attended appointment. This is your core revenue efficiency metric. Calculated.
Earnings Per Appointment = Total Commission ÷ Attended Calls
Earnings Per Call
The average commission earned per booked call — including no-shows. Useful for understanding the value of every scheduled call. Calculated.
Earnings Per Call = Total Commission ÷ Booked Calls
Front End Sale
The total number of front-end purchases made through your funnel during the period (e.g., Genesis purchases). Input column.
Front End Sale Conv Rate
The percentage of leads (or people who saw the offer) who made a front-end purchase. Calculated.
Front End Sale Conv Rate = Front End Sales ÷ Leads
Impressions
The total number of times your ad was shown on screen. Counts every display — the same person seeing it multiple times counts multiple times. Pulled from Meta. Input column.
Lead Conv Rate
The percentage of landing page visitors who became a lead. Calculated.
Lead Conv Rate = Leads ÷ Page Visits (or unique link clicks, depending on your funnel)
Leads
The number of people who submitted their information and became a lead — opt-in, survey submission, or webclass registration. Input column.
Marketing Qualified Leads (MQLs)
Leads who passed your qualification criteria — fit, interest, readiness to buy. Higher quality than a raw opt-in. Input column.
MQL Rate
The percentage of new DMs that became Marketing Qualified Leads. Calculated.
MQL Rate = MQLs ÷ New DMs
My Response Rate
The percentage of inbound DMs that your team responded to. Measures how consistently you're following up with leads. Calculated.
My Response Rate = DM Responses ÷ New DMs
New DMs
The total number of new direct message conversations started as a result of your ads. Counts first messages only — not replies or follow-ups. Input column.
Package
The dollar value of the offer or package the customer purchased (e.g., $1,995 or $7,800). Used to calculate commission. Input column.
Pushed to Webclass
The number of people moved into the webclass portion of the funnel — whether via DM handoff, survey redirect, or manual outreach. Input column.
Saw Offer
The number of people who reached the point in the webclass where your sales offer was presented. Input column.
Saw Offer Conv Rate
The percentage of people who made it far enough to see the offer. Calculated.
Saw Offer Conv Rate = Saw Offer ÷ Pushed to Webclass (or total attendees, depending on your funnel)
Show Rate
The percentage of booked calls that the prospect actually showed up to. Calculated.
Show Rate = Attended ÷ Booked Calls
Spend
The total amount of advertising dollars spent during the reporting period. Pulled from Meta Ads Manager. Input column.
Survey Conv Rate
The percentage of survey page visitors who completed and submitted the survey. Calculated.
Survey Conv Rate = Survey Submissions ÷ Survey Page Visits
Survey Page Visits
The number of people who landed on your survey page. Input column (from GHL).
Survey Submissions
The number of surveys completed and submitted. Input column (from GHL).
Unique Link Clicks
The number of individual people who clicked your ad's link at least once. Removes duplicate clicks from the same person. Pulled from Meta. Input column.
