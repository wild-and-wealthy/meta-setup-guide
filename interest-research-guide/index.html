Interest Research Guide

How to choose your audience interests, how to research them in Meta, and how to test them the right way.

When to use: Before launching your Phase 1 campaign
Time required: 15–20 minutes

01 /

The Rule: Advantage+ Stays On

With Meta's Andromeda update fully rolled out, the algorithm now decides who sees your ad based largely on the content of your creative. Interests have become suggestions — not restrictions.

This means one thing: Advantage+ Detailed Targeting stays on, always. You are not locking Meta into a narrow audience. You are giving it a starting direction and letting it find the right people from there.

The practical approach: Advantage+ ON + 2–3 interest seeds. That's it. Don't overthink the targeting — put your energy into the creative. Your ad is doing the real targeting work.

The interests you add act as a nudge — Meta uses them to calibrate early delivery, then expands as it learns who is actually converting. The better your creative, the less you need to manually guide it.

02 /

The 3 Interest Categories to Focus On

Choose 2–3 interests total — mix across categories. Identity interests are the strongest starting point for most people in this cohort.

Category 1

Identity Interests

Who she IS — how she sees herself

  • Entrepreneurship
  • Small business owner
  • Online business
  • Work from home
  • Female entrepreneur
  • Digital nomad
  • Side hustle
  • Personal development
Category 2

Desire Interests

What she WANTS — the outcome she's after

  • Financial independence
  • Passive income
  • Time freedom
  • Flexible work
  • Life coaching
  • Self-improvement
  • Wealth building
Category 3

Wellness Interests

Use if your ad leans into health/water

  • Health and wellness
  • Holistic health
  • Alternative health
  • Clean living
  • Wellbeing

03 /

What to Avoid

  • "Network marketing" or "Multi-level marketing" — attracts people already in other companies or burned out on the model. Not your person.
  • Single overly broad words like "Health" or "Business" — too much noise, poor targeting signal even as a seed.
  • Competitor brand names — can trigger ad rejection and won't attract the right audience anyway.

04 /

How to Research Interests in Meta Ads Manager

Not every interest you think of will exist as a targetable option in Meta. Always verify before using.

Step 1 — Navigate to Ad Set Level

Open Meta Ads Manager → Create or Edit Campaign → go to the Ad Set level → scroll to Detailed Targeting.

Step 2 — Search Your Seed Keywords

Click into the search bar and type a keyword (e.g., "entrepreneurship"). Meta will show you matching interests and their estimated reach.

Step 3 — Browse Suggestions

After selecting an interest, click Suggestions — Meta will recommend related interests based on what you've already chosen. This is often where you find the best options.

Step 4 — Check Audience Size

For Phase 1 testing, aim for 1M–5M estimated reach per interest. Too small = restricted delivery. Too large = weak signal.

Don't stack interests in one ad set. If you add 8 interests to one ad set, you can't tell which one is working. In Phase 1, keep it to 2–3 max — and if you want to test more, duplicate the ad set rather than adding them all together.

05 /

ChatGPT Prompt for Interest Research

Use this before going into Meta to generate a starting list, then verify each one inside Ads Manager:

"I'm running Meta ads targeting women aged 28–40 who want to build a flexible, income-generating business from home. They value health, personal freedom, and financial independence. They are entrepreneurially-minded but skeptical of traditional MLM.

Give me 30 Facebook interest targeting ideas across three categories:
1. Identity-based interests (who she is)
2. Aspiration/outcome-based interests (what she wants)
3. Health/lifestyle interests

Format as a simple list under each category."

Take the output into Meta Ads Manager and search each suggestion — confirm it exists as a targetable interest and check the estimated audience size before using it.

07 /

The Phase 1 Approach to Interests

1
Keep Advantage+ on This is non-negotiable. You are not restricting your audience — you are giving Meta a starting point.
2
Add 2–3 identity interests as seeds Start with the Identity category — these tend to perform best for this audience. Entrepreneurship + Financial independence + Work from home is a solid Phase 1 starting point.
3
Let it run for 7 days before touching anything The learning phase needs time. Resist the urge to change targeting mid-flight — it resets the algorithm's progress.
4
After 7 days, read your CPL If cost per lead is reasonable and lead quality feels right, stay the course. If results are poor, you can test removing interests entirely and running truly broad in Phase 2.
Your ad is your targeting. The more specific and resonant your creative, the better Meta gets at finding the right person — regardless of what interests you've selected. In Phase 1, the message matters more than the audience settings.
