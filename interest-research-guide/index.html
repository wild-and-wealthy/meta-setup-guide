Interest Research Guide

How to choose your audience interests, what to avoid, and how to research them before you launch.

When to use: Before launching your Phase 1 campaign
Time required: 15–20 minutes

01 /

The Rule: Advantage+ Stays On

With Meta's Andromeda update fully rolled out, the algorithm now decides who sees your ad based largely on the content of your creative. Interests have become suggestions — not restrictions.

This means one thing: Advantage+ Detailed Targeting stays on, always. You are not locking Meta into a narrow audience. You are giving it a starting direction and letting it find the right people from there.

The setup: Advantage+ ON + 1–2 interests as seeds. Pick from the categories below. Don't overthink it — your creative is doing the targeting work.

The interests you add act as a nudge — Meta uses them to calibrate early delivery, then expands as it learns who is converting. The better your creative, the less the interests matter.

02 /

Three Categories of Interests

Every interest falls into one of three buckets. You don't need one from each — pick 1–2 that feel most aligned with your angle.

Category 1

Identity

Who they see themselves as. These people have already claimed the identity — strong signal of the right person.

  • Entrepreneurship
  • Online business
  • Small business owner
  • Work from home
  • Affiliate marketing
  • Digital marketing
Category 2

Desire

What they're actively seeking. Signals intent — they're already looking for a way out or a way forward.

  • Financial independence
  • Work from home
  • Time freedom
  • Flexible work
  • Personal development
  • Wealth building
Category 3

Influence

Who they follow. The strongest signal of all — these people have self-selected into this world. Note: many names won't appear exactly in Meta. Search the name and use what Meta suggests (e.g. "Marketing personalities") — that's still a valid and usable signal.

  • Amy Porterfield
  • Jenna Kutcher
  • Marie Forleo
  • Tony Robbins
  • Russell Brunson
  • Alex Hormozi
  • Dean Graziosi
  • Brendon Burchard
  • Denise Duffield-Thomas
  • Tim Ferriss
  • Mel Robbins
  • Rachel Rodgers

03 /

What to Avoid

These interests attract the wrong type of person, inflate your cost without improving lead quality, or pull in people in browsing mode — not buying mode.

Low buyer intent

  • Side hustle — attracts people looking for extra $20/hour, not a business investment.
  • Passive income — get-rich-quick browsers. Low conversion on education and training offers.
  • Work-life balance — inspiration-seekers, not action-takers. Very low buyer intent despite sounding perfect.
  • Career change — these people want a new job, not a business.
  • Freelancing — trading time for money differently, not building leverage. Wrong model.
  • Digital nomad — primarily dreamers. Aspirational but unlikely to invest.

Content consumers, not buyers

  • Self-improvement / Motivation / Mindfulness — people consuming content, not looking for an offer to invest in.
  • Entrepreneur Magazine / Forbes / Business Insider — readers, not investors.
  • Oprah Winfrey / Rachel Hollis — too broad or too fragmented to give a useful buyer signal.

Wrong model or burned audience

  • Network marketing / Multi-level marketing — attracts people already in competing companies or burned out on the industry entirely.
  • Female empowerment — enormous audience with no business intent. Inflates cost with weak lead quality.
Always confirm an interest exists in Meta before using it. Some that sound right have been quietly removed. Search in Detailed Targeting — if it doesn't appear, it's gone. Move on. ClickFunnels, for example, is not a targetable Meta interest.

04 /

How to Research Interests in Meta Ads Manager

Not every interest you think of will exist as a targetable option in Meta. Always verify before using.

Step 1 — Navigate to Ad Set Level

Open Meta Ads Manager → Create or Edit Campaign → go to the Ad Set level → scroll to Detailed Targeting.

Step 2 — Search Your Keywords

Click into the search bar and type a name or keyword. Meta will show matching interests. If an exact name doesn't appear, look at what Meta suggests instead — the broader category it returns is still usable.

Step 3 — Browse Suggestions

After selecting an interest, click Suggestions — Meta will recommend related interests based on what you've already chosen. This is often where the best options show up.

05 /

AI Prompt for Interest Research

Use any AI tool to brainstorm a starting list, then verify each one inside Ads Manager before using it:

"I run a digital business mentorship that helps people build an income-generating online business. My audience is entrepreneurially-minded but skeptical of traditional MLM.

Give me 20 Facebook interest targeting ideas across two categories:
1. Influencers or public figures my audience likely follows (online business, entrepreneurship, personal finance, or personal development)
2. Topic or intent-based interests (what they're actively searching for — business-building, income, freedom)

Format as a simple list under each category."

Take the output into Meta Ads Manager and search each suggestion. Confirm it exists before using it. If Meta returns a broader category instead of the exact name, that's still valid.

07 /

Phase 1: Set It and Forget It

In Phase 1, you are not testing which interests work best — you are testing which hooks work best. The goal is to find your winning creative. The interests are just giving Meta a head start.

1
Keep Advantage+ on Non-negotiable. You are giving Meta a starting direction, not restricting who it can reach.
2
Add 1–2 interests from the categories above One Influence interest + one Identity or Desire interest is the standard setup. Start with Amy Porterfield + Online business if you're not sure where to begin.
3
Leave it alone Once your ad set is live, don't touch the targeting. The learning phase needs time and data. Changing targeting mid-flight resets the algorithm's progress. Set it, watch your creative performance, and let it run.
Your ad is your targeting. When the hook and message are right, Meta will find the right person. Phase 1 is about finding that hook. Keep the targeting simple and focus your energy on creative.
