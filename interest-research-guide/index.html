Interest Research Guide

How to pick your audience interests, what to avoid, and how to research them before you launch.

When to use: Before launching your Phase 1 campaign
Time required: 15–20 minutes

01 /

The Rule: Advantage+ Stays On

With Meta's Andromeda update fully rolled out, the algorithm now decides who sees your ad based largely on the content of your creative. Interests have become suggestions — not restrictions.

This means one thing: Advantage+ Detailed Targeting stays on, always. You are not locking Meta into a narrow audience. You are giving it a starting direction and letting it find the right people from there.

The setup: Advantage+ ON + 1 influencer interest + 1 intent interest. That's it. Don't overthink the targeting — put your energy into the creative. Your ad is doing the real targeting work.

The interests you add act as a nudge — Meta uses them to calibrate early delivery, then expands as it learns who is actually converting. The better your creative, the less you need to manually guide it.

Two Types of Interests

Pick one from each column — an influencer interest and an intent interest. Add both to your ad set alongside Advantage+.

Type 1

Influencer Interests

People who follow these figures have already self-identified as wanting an online business or a freer life — strong buyer signal.

  • Amy Porterfield
  • Jenna Kutcher
  • Marie Forleo
  • Tony Robbins
  • Russell Brunson
  • Alex Hormozi
  • Dean Graziosi
  • Brendon Burchard
  • Denise Duffield-Thomas
  • Tim Ferriss
  • Mel Robbins
  • Rachel Rodgers
Type 2

Intent Interests

Signal someone actively looking for a solution — not just passively scrolling.

  • Online business
  • Entrepreneurship
  • Financial independence
  • Work from home
  • Digital marketing
  • Affiliate marketing
Important: many influencer names won't appear exactly in Meta. When you search a name, Meta may return a broader category instead — for example, searching "Amy Porterfield" returns "Marketing personalities (marketing)." That broader category is still a strong signal and is fine to use. Just be aware you're targeting everyone in that category, not only that person's followers.

03 /

What to Avoid

These interests attract the wrong type of person, inflate your CPM without improving lead quality, or pull in people in browsing mode rather than buying mode.

Wrong buyer intent

  • Side hustle — attracts $20/hr gig seekers. Low commitment, high churn.
  • Passive income — get-rich-quick browsers. Low conversion on training-based offers.
  • Work-life balance — sounds perfect but performs poorly. Inspiration-seekers, not action-takers. Very low buyer intent.
  • Self-improvement / Motivation / Mindfulness — content consumers, not offer converters.
  • Career change — these people want a new job, not a business.
  • Digital nomad / Freelancing — aspirational dreamers or trade-time-for-money types. Wrong model.

Too vague or too broad

  • Female empowerment — enormous audience with no business intent. Inflates CPM with weak lead quality.
  • Entrepreneur Magazine / Forbes / Business Insider — content consumers, not offer converters.
  • Oprah Winfrey — too broad. No meaningful buyer signal.

Wrong model associations

  • Network marketing / Multi-level marketing — attracts people already in other companies or burned out on the model.
  • Rachel Hollis — fragmented post-controversy. Unpredictable performance.
Always confirm an interest exists before using it. Some interests that sound right have been quietly removed by Meta. Search inside Ads Manager before building around it — if it doesn't appear, it's gone. ClickFunnels, for example, is not a targetable Meta interest.

04 /

How to Research Interests in Meta Ads Manager

Not every interest you think of will exist as a targetable option in Meta. Always verify before using.

Step 1 — Navigate to Ad Set Level

Open Meta Ads Manager → Create or Edit Campaign → go to the Ad Set level → scroll to Detailed Targeting.

Step 2 — Search Your Keywords

Click into the search bar and type a name or keyword. Meta will show you matching interests. If an exact name doesn't appear, look at what Meta suggests instead — the category bucket it returns is still usable.

Step 3 — Browse Suggestions

After selecting an interest, click Suggestions — Meta will recommend related interests based on what you've already chosen. This is often where you find unexpected strong options.

05 /

Use AI to Generate Ideas

Use an AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) to brainstorm a starting list, then verify each one inside Ads Manager:

"I run a digital business mentorship that helps people build an income-generating online business. My audience is entrepreneurially-minded but skeptical of traditional MLM.

Give me 20 Facebook interest targeting ideas across two categories:
1. Influencers or public figures my audience likely follows (online business, entrepreneurship, personal finance, or personal development)
2. Topic or intent-based interests (what they're actively searching for or interested in — business-building, income, freedom)

Format as a simple list under each category."

Take the output into Meta Ads Manager and search each suggestion. Confirm it exists as a targetable interest before using it. The category Meta returns is fine even if the exact name doesn't match.
