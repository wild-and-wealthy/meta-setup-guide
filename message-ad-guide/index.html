Message Ad Setup Guide

How to automatically send your opt-in link the moment someone messages your ad — no manual follow-up needed.

Time: 5–10 minutes Two options: ManyChat (recommended) or Meta Native Done once per ad
Before you start: Make sure your message ad is already created and active in Meta Ads Manager. This guide covers automation only — not how to build the ad itself.

Why Your Current Setup Isn't Firing

If you set up ManyChat to respond when someone types the word "ready" — that's the issue. Keyword triggers are unreliable for message ads.

Here's why: when someone clicks a message ad, Meta doesn't send a free-form message. It sends a system-generated opener — a pre-populated prompt or "Get Started" tap — that ManyChat doesn't recognize as your keyword. So the automation never fires, and the lead gets nothing.

The fix is simple: switch from a keyword trigger to ManyChat's Ads Entry Point trigger. It fires the moment someone opens a conversation from your ad — no keyword required, no drop-off.

The short version Keyword triggers = broken for message ads. Ads Entry Point trigger = works every time.

02 /

Option 1 — ManyChat (Recommended)

This is the right long-term setup. Once connected to your ad, ManyChat delivers the link instantly and lets you build follow-up sequences, tag leads, and automate the full conversation — all hands-free.

Step 1 — Log into ManyChat

Go to:

→ app.manychat.com

Log in to your account and make sure you're connected to the correct Facebook Page.

Step 2 — Create a New Flow

In the left sidebar, click Automation, then click + New Flow.

Give it a clear name so you can find it later — something like Ad — Opt-In Link Delivery.

Step 3 — Set the Trigger
  1. Click + Add Trigger at the top of the flow
  2. Search for and select "Facebook Ads JSON" — it may also appear as "Ads Entry Point" depending on your ManyChat version
  3. Click Connect Ad and select the specific ad you want this flow to fire from
  4. Save the trigger
✓ Connect the ad, not the campaign Make sure you select the individual ad — not the campaign or ad set. The trigger must be tied to the exact ad for it to fire correctly.
Step 4 — Build the First Message
  1. Click the first message block in the flow
  2. Write a short, warm message with your link — example: "Hey! Here's your link to get started → [YOUR OPT-IN PAGE LINK]"
  3. Keep it one or two lines — the goal is speed, not length
Step 5 — Publish the Flow
  1. Click Publish in the top right corner
  2. Test it: click your ad from a personal account (not your business account) and confirm the link arrives automatically
✅ If the link arrives instantly — you're set.
Your leads will now receive the opt-in link the moment they tap to message, with zero manual action on your end.

Troubleshooting ManyChat

If you've followed the steps and the flow still isn't firing, work through this list before assuming something is broken on Meta's end.

What's happening Most likely cause Fix
Flow doesn't fire at all Flow is saved as a draft, not published Open the flow and click Publish in the top right — draft mode means it's visible but inactive
Flow fires for some people, not others Trigger is connected to the campaign or ad set instead of the specific ad Edit the trigger and re-select — go all the way down to the individual ad level
ManyChat isn't seeing your ads ManyChat is connected to the wrong Facebook Page Go to Settings → Connected Accounts and confirm it's the Page your ads run from
Test works, but real leads don't get the message Testing from your own account (Meta blocks self-testing) Ask a team member or a trusted contact to click the live ad and confirm receipt
Still stuck after all of this? Reach out before spending more on traffic. Don't run a campaign on a broken flow — confirm it's working end-to-end first.

Option 2 — Meta Native Instant Reply

If you're not using ManyChat or just need something live today, Meta has a built-in auto-reply that requires zero integrations. It's not as precise — it sends to everyone who first messages your Page, not just people from a specific ad — but it works and it's fast to set up.

The tradeoff This option sends one automatic reply to any new conversation on your Page — not just from your ad. You also can't build follow-up sequences from it. Use it as a temporary bridge while you get ManyChat sorted, not as a long-term solution.
Step 1 — Go to Meta Business Suite

Open:

→ business.facebook.com

Make sure you're in the correct business account before continuing.

Step 2 — Open Inbox Settings

In the left menu, click Inbox. Then look for the Settings gear icon — usually in the top right of the Inbox view — and click it.

Step 3 — Find Instant Reply

Look for "Instant Reply" or "Automated Responses" in the settings list. Toggle it ON.

Step 4 — Write Your Message

Type your automated reply. Keep it brief and include the link right up front. Example:

"Thanks for reaching out! Here's the link you requested: [YOUR OPT-IN PAGE LINK] — feel free to message me if you have any questions!"

Step 5 — Save and Test
  1. Click Save
  2. Message your Page from a personal account (not your business account) to confirm the reply sends correctly
✓ One-time note Instant Reply only fires the first time someone messages your Page. If they've ever messaged you before, they won't receive it again.

Which Option Should You Use?

Here's a quick side-by-side to help you decide.

Option 1: ManyChat Option 2: Meta Native
Sends link automatically Yes Yes
Tied to a specific ad Yes No — fires for all Page messages
Can follow up with more messages Yes No
Requires a ManyChat account Yes No
Setup time 10 min 5 min
Recommendation
If you have ManyChat, use Option 1 — it's more precise, more powerful, and the right long-term setup. If you're just getting started and want something live today, use Option 2 as a bridge while you get ManyChat configured.
