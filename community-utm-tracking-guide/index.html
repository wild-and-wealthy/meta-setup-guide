Wild & Wealthy — UTM Tracking Guide

UTM Tracking Setup Guide

See exactly which campaign, ad set, and ad is driving your leads — directly inside GHL.

Platform: GHL (GoHighLevel) only
Ad type: Landing page ads only
Time required: 10 minutes
When to set up: After your campaign is live
⚠ GHL users only These instructions are specific to GoHighLevel. If you are using a different funnel platform, this guide does not apply to your setup. This also applies to landing page ads only — send message ads and instant form ads do not use URL parameters in the same way.

01 /

What UTM Tracking Does

UTM codes are short tags you add to your ad's destination URL. When someone clicks your ad and opts in, those tags pass through to GHL and get attached to the contact's record — so you can see exactly which ad brought them in.

Without UTMs

You know leads are coming in. You don't know which of your 15 ads is responsible. Meta's attribution data is approximate at best.

With UTMs

Every contact in GHL shows which campaign, ad set, and ad brought them in. You can see at a glance which creative is working.

Focus on campaign name + ad name. In Phase 1, you're running one campaign and one ad set — so those are redundant. The ad name is what tells you which of your 15 hooks drove each lead.

02 /

Step 1 — Add the URL Parameter in Meta

This is added at the ad level inside Meta Ads Manager — not on your funnel or in GHL.

Where to find it

Meta Ads Manager → your campaign → Ad level → scroll to Destination → expand URL Parameters

Paste this exactly

Do not change anything in this string. Meta fills in the dynamic values automatically for each individual ad:

utm_source=facebook&utm_medium={{adset.name}}&utm_campaign={{campaign.name}}&utm_content={{ad.name}}&campaign_id={{campaign.id}}
The double curly braces are intentional. {{campaign.name}}, {{adset.name}}, and {{ad.name}} are Meta dynamic parameters — Meta replaces them automatically with the real values when the ad runs. Do not replace them manually.

03 /

Step 2 — Map the Values in GHL

Once the URL parameters are in place, GHL needs to know where to store that data when a lead comes in. You do this by mapping the UTM values to fields in your lead sheet or contact view.

Add these field mappings in GHL

In your GHL lead form, automation, or contact view, add these three fields:

Campaign Name
{{contact.attributionSource.utmCampaign}}
Ad Set Name
{{contact.attributionSource.utmMedium}}
Ad Name
{{contact.attributionSource.utmContent}}

These can be added as columns to any existing lead tracking worksheet or contact view inside GHL.

In Phase 1, ad name is your most valuable field. Since you're running all ads in one campaign and one ad set, the campaign name and ad set name will be identical for every lead. The ad name is what differentiates — it tells you which hook brought each person in.

04 /

What to Know Before You Read the Data

GHL captures first attribution

GHL records where a lead came from the first time they entered your funnel. If the same person clicks a different ad three months later and opts in again, GHL will still show the original source — not the most recent one.

This is called first-touch attribution. It's not a bug — it's just how GHL works. Keep it in mind when reading your contact records, especially for leads who may have been in your funnel before.

Send message ads — does not apply

Send message ads open a Messenger conversation — there is no destination URL, so UTM parameters don't apply. These leads are not trackable via UTMs.

Instant form ads — does not apply

Instant form ads load entirely within Facebook — the lead never visits your funnel URL. UTM tracking does not apply to instant form ads.

05 /

When to Set This Up

UTM tracking is not required to launch your ads. Your priority in Session 5 is getting your campaign live. UTMs are a layer on top that makes your data more readable inside GHL.

Recommended timing: Set up UTMs when you're inside Meta building your campaign, before you publish. It takes 10 minutes and you only have to do it once per campaign.

If you've already launched without UTMs, you can add them to existing ads — just be aware that data won't be retroactive for leads who came in before the parameters were added.
